Wire

Motion Guideline

  • Head of Motion Design
  • 2014 to present
  • wire.com

Wire is a modern communications network. A startup backed by Janus Friis, co-founder of Skype.

As the sole UI Motion Designer in a team of visual and interaction designers I’m responsible to lead the creation of Wire's motion guideline. A reference document that synthesises the reasoning behind the transitions and behaviour of Wire user interface and provides a consistent language for the application along different platforms and devices.

The guideline contains a set of prototypes covering the most important interactions of the application. It’s conceived as a living document that will grow and update as the product continues to develop new features and explore new interactions.

Nokia

N9 Motion Guideline

  • UI Motion Designer & Prototyper
  • 2011

Harmattan was a Meego based OS developed by Nokia. It was going to be first implemented in Nokia’s N9 device, which aimed to be the company’s entry into the competitive high-end smartphone market.

I was part of a small team of interaction and visual designers. My role as a UI Motion Designer consisted of translating graphic proposals made by the visual design team into functioning prototypes to help breathe life into the interface components and improve the overall user’s experience.

Our final goal was to define the motion guidelines that serve as reference when developing new components for the operating system, native and third party applications. To do this we had to assure homogeneity and coherence throughout the whole operating system.

Nokia

Future of Icons

  • Concept & Prototyper
  • 2009

The objective of this project was to explore several concepts concerning the role icons would have on future devices. The context for the research and experimentation was not constrained to any type of device or technology in specific. It’s goal was to create a range of concepts that would generate new questions and inspire further investigation.

The project was done by a small team of 3 visual and interaction designers including myself. I was part of the brainstorming sessions and also responsible for creating visuals and prototypes to explored our ideas.
